Bio

Zachary Viet Pine (b. 1993) is a composer and sousaphonist working in New Orleans.

Zach composes with the spiritual devotion of a California hippy. This approach is jointly informed by four psychedelic years in Berkeley and the teachings of his Zen practitioner uncle in Little Saigon. His music is colored by a sawari palette of breaths and bows. In this mu landscape, digital manipulation and overt quotation remind us of the listener's increasingly commodified attention.

From 2018 to 2021, Zach studied with Max Dulaney and Rick Snow. His work has been performed by Mivos Quartet, Ensemble LINEA The Rhythm Method, and Bent Duo.

Zach also enjoys popular music as arranger, sousaphonist and band-leader. He arranges groovy tunes for his Sousaphone and Percussion duo, Noodle. He writes tight vocal harmonies to classic folk songs for himself and his friends in the St. Charles String Beans. He also creates custom arrangements of pop songs for ensembles in D.C. and New Orleans.
